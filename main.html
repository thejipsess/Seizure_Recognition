The data used here is EEG data. Originally there were 500 participants, who were recorded for 23.5 seconds with 4097 data points per sample. Each sample was divided into 23 chunks , each containing 178 data points for 1 second. These chunks were shuffled, resulting in 11500 samples.

5 - (healthy, surface recording) eyes open, means when they were recording the EEG signal of the brain the patient had their eyes open 4 - (healthy, surface recording) eyes closed, means when they were recording the EEG signal the patient had their eyes closed 3 - (epiliptic, intracranial recording) The EEG activity from the hippocampal formation in the non-epiletogenic hemisphere with no seizure 2 - (healthy, surface recording) The EEG activity from the epileptogenic zone brain area with no seizure 1 - (healthy, surface recording) Recording of seizure activity

# Get rid of the most extreme outlier
row_max <- which(x == max(x), arr.ind = TRUE)[1]
y <- y[-row_max]
x <- x[-row_max,]


# Remove univariate outliers
for(i in seq(1, 178)){
  Q1 <- quantile(x[,i], 0.25)
  Q3 <- quantile(x[,i], 0.75)
  IQR <- Q3 - Q1
  uplim <- Q3 + 10*IQR
  downlim <- Q1 - 10*IQR
  
  y <- y[x[names(x)[i]] <= uplim & x[names(x)[i]] >= downlim]
  x <- x[x[names(x)[i]] <= uplim & x[names(x)[i]] >= downlim,]
}
